Koshari Recipe

Description

Many koshari recipes only have a cooked red vinegar sauce. However, this recipe has two types of red sauce: a fresh red sauce and a cooked vinegar red sauce.

This recipe has been perfected over a lifetime, and the fresh red sauce is the secret sauce.

Ingredients

  • rice
  • lentils
  • onions
  • oil
  • salt & pepper
  • cumin
  • coriander
  • suger
  • tomatoes
  • tomatoe paste
  • vinegar
  • red pepper
  • garlic
  • ghee
  • pasta: spaghetti & elbows
  • lime
  • Chickpeas
  • bell pepper
  • flour

Steps

  1. Boil the chickpeas with cumin while preparing the fried onions.

    Once the chickpeas are tender, add a squeeze of lime on top.

  2. Chop the onions into thin circles. Allow the onions tossed in flour to rest for 30 minutes prior to frying.

    Fry the onions in oil until golden brown, then put them on a paper towel lined plate.

  3. Add the ingredients for the optional fresh red sauce to a food processor.
  4. Blend on high. Set aside.
  5. Blend the peeled onions in the food processor.
  6. Add some oil to a high-rimmed pot and fry the blended onions for about 10 minutes on medium-low heat.
  7. Add the soaked brown lentils and water to the cooked onions. Bring to a boil for 10 minutes, or until the lentils are soft.

  8. Turn down the heat to medium-low and add the rice and more water. Add salt and pepper and mix the rice.

    Cover and cook for about 12 to 15 minutes, or until the rice is steamed. Remove from the heat and keep the rice covered.

  9. Cook the pasta until al dente per the package instructions while making the tomato vinegar chili sauce.
  10. Blend the tomatoes in a blender. Pour the pureed tomatoes into a strainer.
  11. Use a spoon to press the blended tomatoes through the strainer.

  12. n a medium saucepan on high heat, add some oil and fry the red pepper flakes (or cayenne pepper) and minced garlic for about 1 minute.

    Add the tomato paste and white vinegar for about 30 seconds.

  13. Add the strained tomatoes. Stir in the ground cumin, ground coriander, salt, sugar, and ground pepper.
  14. Boil on medium-high heat for about 10 minutes. Add a chili pepper if desired.
  15. Assemble your koshari bowl!
